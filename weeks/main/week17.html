Week 17: Portfolio Presentation & Evaluation

Introduction

With projects completed, Week 17 focuses on compiling your work into a portfolio presentation and conducting a self- and peer-evaluation. Document your process and reflect on your projectâ€™s success and areas for improvement.

Portfolio Preparation

Your portfolio should include:

  • Design Brief & Planning: The initial task, constraints, and inspiration.
  • Development & Process: Detailed design drawings, photos of construction, and captions explaining each step.
  • Challenges & Solutions: A description of at least one challenge and how you solved it.
  • Safety Considerations: How you maintained safety throughout the project.
  • Final Outcome: Photos and specifications of your finished mirror frame.
  • Improvements: Reflections on what you would do differently next time.

Presentation Skills

Each student (or group) will present their project to the class. Practice clear, confident communication covering your design intent, construction process, and lessons learnt.

Evaluation (Self and Peer)

Evaluate your work by assessing quality, aesthetics, functionality, and process management. Use both self-assessment and peer feedback to identify strengths and areas for improvement.

Student Activity

Complete the Project Evaluation form on Classroom, and upload your final portfolio file (or submit a physical copy) as required.

Conclusion

Week 17 is about reflection and communication. Evaluating and presenting your project helps consolidate your learning and builds confidence in your skills.
