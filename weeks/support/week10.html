Week 10: Finishing Design Elements & Workshop Maintenance

Introduction

By Week 10, we are roughly at the half-way point of our project timeline. This week is about wrapping up any remaining design elements on your mirror frame and ensuring the workshop and tools are well-maintained for the next stages. Think of it as a checkpoint: finish all modifications before moving into the finishing (sanding, painting/oiling) phase, and do some mid-project workshop maintenance to keep our environment safe and efficient.

Complete Your Design Features

Take this time to finalise any decorative work on your mirror frame:

  • If you planned a pattern or carving and haven‚Äôt done it yet, do it now. Once we begin sanding and finishing, adding new cuts or carvings will mean re-sanding/re-finishing those areas, which is less efficient.
  • Double-check symmetry and consistency. For instance, if you routed an edge profile on one part of the frame last week, ensure all corresponding parts get the same treatment now so the frame looks uniform.
  • Sand any rough edges around decorative cuts. Use small files or sandpaper on a block to smooth out carved details without losing the definition.
  • Test-fit the mirror glass (without permanently fixing it) if your frame is assembled. It should fit into the rebate or opening comfortably. If not, carefully adjust the opening size now (lightly trim with a chisel or sand the inside edges) rather than forcing the glass later.

Perform a thorough quality check on your woodworking: all joints are secure (if something is still unglued, plan to glue it soon), all design features done, no cracks or split pieces (if something cracked, consult on how to fix or replace that piece now).

Workshop Maintenance

A clean and well-maintained workshop ensures the second half of our project goes smoothly:

  • Tool Maintenance: Are the hand tools and power tools in good shape? This is a good time to sharpen chisels or plane irons (dull tools can cause accidents and poor work). The instructor may demonstrate quick sharpening techniques. If the router bit used last week has resin buildup, it should be cleaned.   • Cleanup: Remove clutter from benches. Sweep or vacuum dust and wood chips from machines and floor. Piles of sawdust are not only a fire hazard but can also affect the quality of finishing.
  • Storage: Stack or store your project pieces safely. Have a designated area where they lie flat or stand securely. Keep your mirror glass stored safely to avoid damage.
  • Inventory: Check consumables ‚Äì are we low on sandpaper, glue, or finishing supplies? This helps plan for the next term.

This maintenance session echoes what happens in industry: regular cleanup and tool upkeep improves safety and work quality. It‚Äôs easier to do great work in a tidy shop.
