Week 11: Frame Assembly & Timber Seasoning

Introduction

In Week 11, the mirror frame should come together fully. If not already done, this week is for the final assembly/gluing of the mirror frame and ensuring it’s structurally complete. While the glue dries, we will learn about Timber Seasoning – the process of drying wood – and why it’s important for our projects.

Final Assembly / Gluing the Frame

By now, you have dry-fitted and know your frame pieces fit. Now it’s time for the real glue-up:

  • Gather all needed items before applying glue: clamps (with pads), glue bottle, a small brush, a damp rag, a square, and clamp blocks/cauls.
  • Apply an even layer of wood glue to all joint surfaces that will mate. Use just enough glue for a continuous film.
  • Assemble the frame promptly. If using biscuits or dowels, insert them before the glue skins over.
  • Clamp the frame gently at first, then tighten gradually while checking alignment and squareness.
  • Immediately wipe off excess glue with a damp rag.
  • Ensure the frame is flat and square, then leave clamped until the glue cures.

Timber Seasoning

Fresh-cut wood (green wood) is not used directly for furniture because it must be seasoned – dried to an appropriate moisture content. Key points about seasoning include:

  • Purpose: Reduces moisture to a stable level (usually 8-12% for indoor furniture) to prevent warping or shrinking.
  • Methods: Wood can be air-dried (stacked with spacers) or kiln-dried (using controlled heat and airflow).
  • Signs of Unseasoned Wood: Warping, cracking, or end checking.
  • Relevance: Our project wood is pre-seasoned, but understanding the process is essential for future projects.

For more details, read the article on Versace Timbers: Advantages of Timber Seasoning.
