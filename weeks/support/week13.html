Week 13: Finishing Techniques & Environmental Sustainability

Introduction

Week 13 marks the start of the finishing stage for your mirror project. A well-applied finish not only beautifies but also protects the wood. We’ll explore various finishing techniques and discuss environmental sustainability in our methods.

Types of Wood Finishes

Common finishing products include:

  • Oil Finishes: e.g., linseed oil or Danish oil; they penetrate wood for a natural look.
  • Polyurethane Varnish: Forms a durable film; available in glossy, satin, or matte.
  • Shellac: A traditional finish offering a warm glow.
  • Wax: Often applied on top of other finishes for extra sheen.
  • Paint: An option if a coloured frame is desired.

For our mirror frames, a simple oil or wipe-on poly is ideal for maintaining the natural wood look.

Finishing Technique and Safety

  • Stir/Shake: Ensure finishes are mixed well without bubbles.
  • Test on Scrap: Always test your finish first.
  • Apply Evenly: Use a quality brush or cloth, working with the grain.
  • Wipe Off Excess: Remove surplus finish to avoid sticky surfaces.
  • Ventilation & Safety: Work in a well-ventilated area and use gloves.
  • Drying Time: Respect the product’s drying times.
  • Between Coats: Lightly sand between coats for smoothness.

Environmental Sustainability in Woodworking

  • Sustainably Sourced Timber: Use wood from responsibly managed forests.
  • Efficient Use of Wood: Plan cuts to reduce waste.
  • Low-VOC Finishes: Choose water-based or natural finishes when possible.
  • Safe Disposal: Dispose of chemicals and rags properly.
  • Reuse and Upcycling: Repurpose offcuts and repair instead of replacing.
