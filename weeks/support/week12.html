Week 12: Sanding, Milling Timber & Surface Prep

Introduction

With assembled frames in hand, Week 12 is all about preparing the surface for finishing. This includes thorough sanding and any last touch-ups. We also revisit milling timber – preparing raw lumber for use in your project.

Sanding and Surface Preparation

Before any finish goes on, the wood must be properly sanded and clean. Follow these steps:

  • Start with Medium Grit: Use 120 grit to smooth tool marks and minor imperfections.
  • Progress to Finer Grits: Move to 180 then 240 grit for a smooth finish. Always sand with the grain.
  • Detail Work: Use a sanding block for corners and routed profiles to maintain shape.
  • End Grain: Sand end grain extra carefully.
  • Final Clean: Remove all dust with a vacuum or tack cloth.

Milling Timber in the Workshop

Industrial milling involves:

  1. Jointing: Flatten one face and straighten an edge.
  2. Planing/Thicknessing: Achieve uniform thickness with a planer.
  3. Rip and Crosscut: Cut the board to width and length.

This sequence ensures each piece is square and true, making assembly easier.

Safety and Cleanliness During Sanding

  • Wear a dust mask or respirator.
  • Ensure proper ventilation.
  • Secure small pieces when using power sanders.
