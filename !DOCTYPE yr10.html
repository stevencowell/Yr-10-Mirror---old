Course Overview

Welcome to the Year 10 Timber Technology course. This Stage 5 program builds upon your Year 9 foundations by exploring advanced woodworking projects, including the Mirror and Bedside Table. You’ll refine your design, joinery, and finishing skills, while focusing on safety and sustainable practices.

Syllabus Breakdown

This syllabus covers advanced topics in workshop safety, timber properties, joinery techniques, and sustainable design. Each term focuses on a different project or set of skills:

  • Term 1: Mirror Unit
  • Term 2: Advanced Joinery & Project Planning
  • Term 3: Bedside Table Unit
  • Term 4: Final Project Completion & Evaluation

Assessments

You’ll be assessed on both practical skills and theoretical understanding:

  • Practical Projects: Safe and accurate completion of woodworking tasks.
  • Portfolio Work: Documentation of design processes, planning, and reflections.
  • Class Tests: Understanding of workshop safety, timber properties, and design theory.

Please refer to the course handbook for detailed rubrics and important deadlines.

Mirror Unit

Summary: Mirror | Stage 5 | Technology
Duration: 20 weeks (Term 1, 40-minute lessons, 4 periods per week)

Unit Overview

The Mirror Unit (Term 1) focuses on designing and building a mirror frame. Students will develop advanced joinery techniques, use jigs, and explore finishing methods to create a personalised mirror.

Outcomes & Assessment Overview

  • IND51 – Manage WHS issues and risks.
  • IND52 – Apply design principles to modify and produce projects.
  • IND53 – Use hand/machine tools effectively.
  • IND54 – Justify material selection.
  • IND55 – Communicate design ideas effectively.
  • IND56 – Engage in collaborative work practices.
  • IND57 – Transfer skills across contexts.
  • IND58 – Evaluate products (functional, economic, aesthetic, environmental).
  • IND59 – Analyse emerging timber technologies.
  • IND510 – Assess technology’s impact on society/environment.

Assessment: Practical 25%, Folio 5%, Class Test 10%

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) & Tools

This week, you’ll revisit the essential PPE for cutting, planing, and sanding. Keep your workspace organized to reduce slip or trip hazards.
